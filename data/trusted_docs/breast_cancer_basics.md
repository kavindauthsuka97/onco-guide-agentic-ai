# Breast Cancer Basics

Breast cancer can sometimes present with a new breast lump, nipple discharge, skin dimpling, nipple inversion, swelling, or breast pain.

Not every breast lump is cancer. Some lumps are benign. However, a new breast lump should be checked by a healthcare professional.

Red flag symptoms may include a new lump, bloody nipple discharge, skin changes, unexplained weight loss, or swelling in the armpit.

This information is for education only and does not replace medical advice from a doctor.
